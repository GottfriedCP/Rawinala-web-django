WELCOME TO RAWINALA

We are an institution that serves the educational needs for children with multiple disabilities, for especially a visual impairment. Children with multiple disabilities are difficult to get a special education, even in a special school.

EDUCATION

for children aged 0–20 years with multiple disabilities

EARLY EDUCATION

An education program for children aged 0–6 years. Their initial potential starts to be observed, explored, and developed through many programs adjusted to the children's needs and abilities.

BASIC EDUCATION

This program is designed from four main functional education areas: to live, to work, to play, and to love. Children are directed to develop their potential and to explore other abilities that can support their independence.

ADVANCED EDUCATION

Further education program for children aged 14–18 years to develop work skills according to their needs and abilities. The goal is not only to be able to earn income, but also to be able to do various simple jobs.

CARING

for people with multiple disabilities, which for some reasons, cannot live with their family

REFORMATORY

Provided for school-age children who fit the certain categories.

NURSING HOME

We provide a place of residence for elderly people with multiple disabilities who no longer have family. Each dormitory has a role in daily housework and is assisted by several assistants who work together to build a functional and happy residential environment.

SHELTERED WORKSHOP

Workshops for the children who have completed the further education. Those who are included in the train-able category will be given a job that suits their abilities, so that they can have a salary to meet their daily needs.

COMMUNITY-BASED REHABILITATION

We also serve persons with disabilities outside our institution by empowering communities, other institutions, and the government locally, regionally and nationally.

TRAINING CENTER

The aim of this program is to encourage and to train the educational institutions, both public and private, to open similar services. The targets of this program are teachers, social workers, families, communities, and government.
